Biography

About

Yuexuan (Joyce) Wang has performed as both a soloist and collaborative pianist across China, the United States, Spain, and Italy. Her performances have taken place in distinguished venues including the Shanghai Oriental Art Center, the National Music Hall of China in Beijing, Hongtai Music Hall in Xiamen, the Parsons Music Art Center in Wuhan, Auer Hall and Recital Hall at the Indiana University Jacobs School of Music, and the Concert Hall at Boston University. In addition to her solo performances, she served as collaborative pianist for the Boston University Symphonic Chorus during the 2024–25 season. Since Fall 2025, she has taught group piano courses at Boston University for both music majors and non-music majors, and is also the collaborative pianist for the children's choir at the Old South Church in Boston. Wang has received top prizes in several competitions, including Shanghai International Youth Piano Competition, the National Steinway Piano Competition (China), the Colorado International Piano Academy Competition, the Medici International Music Competition, and the London International Music Competition.

Wang began her musical journey at the age of five under the guidance of her mother, a soprano and choral conductor specializing in Chinese folk music. She is currently pursuing the Doctor of Musical Arts degree at Boston University under the tutelage of pianist Boaz Sharon. Her previous studies include a Master of Music degree and Performer Diploma from the Indiana University Jacobs School of Music, where she studied with distinguished pianists Arnaldo Cohen, winner of the Ferruccio Busoni International Piano Competition; Émile Naoumoff, the last disciple of Nadia Boulanger; and Edward Auer, laureate of the International Chopin Piano Competition. She earned her Bachelor's degree from the China Conservatory of Music in Beijing, where she studied with Singaporean pianist JingJing Lim. During her studies at Indiana University, she also worked with harpsichordist and fortepianist Hsuan Chang Kitano, exploring historical keyboard instruments, and studied choral conducting with Gwyn Richards.

In addition to her formal education, Wang has participated in numerous festivals and educational programs, including the Virtuoso & Belcanto Festival in Lucca, Italy; the Gijón International Piano Festival in Gijón, Spain; the Edward Auer Workshop in Bloomington, Indiana; and the Colorado International Piano Academy in Greeley, Colorado. She has participated in masterclasses with distinguished pianists including Stanislav Ioudenitch, Craig Sheppard, Norman Krieger, Ran Dank, Till Fellner, and Jeffrey Cohen. Wang also served as an assistant teacher at the Shanghai FYM International Music Festival.

Outside of music, Wang enjoys listening to podcasts and exploring crafting games, finding creativity and relaxation in virtual worlds as much as at the piano. She currently lives in Boston with her beloved cat, Frida, whose curious and independent spirit brings her daily joy and inspiration.
